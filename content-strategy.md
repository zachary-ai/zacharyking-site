# Content Strategy - zacharyking.com.au

**Purpose:** Establish authority in AI-enabled GTM and fractional execution while driving inbound leads for both Fractional GTM engagements and FEC membership.

**Target Audiences:**
1. **Founders** (Primary): B2B SaaS, pre-seed to Series A/B, post-funding, 5-30 employees, need GTM infrastructure
2. **Fractional Operators** (Secondary): Experienced sales/GTM operators building or scaling fractional practices

**Content Flywheel:**
```
Client work → Build AI systems → Extract insights → Publish content →
Drive authority → Generate inbound → Better clients → [loop]
```

---

## Content Pillars

### Pillar 1: GTM Infrastructure (Founders)

**Positioning:** Modern GTM is about systems, not heroics. Founders need repeatable engines, not more advice.

**Core message:** Stop trying to hire your way out of GTM chaos. Build infrastructure first.

**Target reader:** Technical/product founders overwhelmed by sales, post-funding, needing to professionalize.

---

### Pillar 2: Fractional Model Advocacy (Both Audiences)

**Positioning:** Fractional is the optimal model for early-stage execution and experienced operator leverage.

**Core message:** Full-time is overrated. Fractional unlocks speed, expertise, and flexibility without the commitment cost.

**Target reader:** Founders considering fractional vs full-time hire, operators exploring fractional transition.

---

### Pillar 3: AI + Operations (Both Audiences)

**Positioning:** AI is infrastructure, not a gimmick. It's how modern operators scale without headcount.

**Core message:** The best operators aren't afraid of AI—they're building competitive moats with it.

**Target reader:** Operators wanting to differentiate, founders wanting leverage without complexity.

---

### Pillar 4: Founder Reality (Founders)

**Positioning:** Real talk about the messy, human side of building GTM engines as a founder.

**Core message:** You're not broken. Founder-led sales is supposed to feel hard. Here's how to move through it.

**Target reader:** Founders feeling stuck, overwhelmed, or behind on GTM maturity.

---

### Pillar 5: Operator Craft (Fractionals)

**Positioning:** Fractional execution is a skill. Here's how the best operators structure, deliver, and scale their practices.

**Core message:** Going fractional isn't just "consulting but less." It's a distinct craft with its own playbook.

**Target reader:** Operators considering fractional, early fractionals finding their footing, experienced fractionals optimizing.

## Voice & Style Guidelines

### Tone

**What we are:**
- Direct and clear (no fluff)
- Confident but not arrogant
- Empathetic to founder struggles
- Pragmatic and systems-oriented
- Opinionated with conviction

**What we're not:**
- Corporate or formal
- Overly technical or jargon-heavy
- Sales-y or promotional
- Generic or bland
- Apologetic or hedging

### Writing Principles

1. **Lead with insight, not introduction**
   - Don't waste first 200 words on setup
   - Start with bold claim or insight
   - Hook readers immediately

2. **Use real examples**
   - Client stories (anonymized if needed)
   - Personal experiences
   - Specific numbers and outcomes
   - Avoid hypotheticals

3. **Make it scannable**
   - Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences max)
   - Descriptive subheadings
   - Bullet points and numbered lists
   - Bold key phrases

4. **Show, don't tell**
   - Don't say "I'm an expert"
   - Demonstrate expertise through insight
   - Let examples prove the point

5. **End with clear action**
   - Every article ends with CTA
   - Make next step obvious
   - Don't be afraid to ask

### Vocabulary

**Use these words:**
- Systems, infrastructure, engine
- Signal, leverage, repeatability
- Embedded, execution, outcomes
- Founder-enabled, AI-powered
- Fractional (not consultant)

**Avoid these words:**
- Synergy, ecosystem, disrupt
- Consultant, advisor (unless explicitly contrasting)
- Revolutionary, game-changer
- Guru, expert, thought leader (self-referential)
